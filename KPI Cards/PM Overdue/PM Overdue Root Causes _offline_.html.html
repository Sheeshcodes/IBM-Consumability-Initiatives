Maximo · Diagnostic guide

Why PM overdue metrics drift above target — and how to fix them

When the preventive maintenance backlog starts climbing past its threshold in Maximo, the cause is rarely a single issue. This guide walks through the eight categories of root cause to investigate, in roughly the order a planner or reliability engineer should rule them out.

Updated May 26, 2026 · 14 min read · Reviewed by reliability engineers

The root cause is usually a combination

Treat the categories below as a checklist. Two or three overlapping factors — say, a stuck PMWOGEN cron task plus a labor shortage — produce the same backlog signature, so resist diagnosing on the first hit.

Resource constraints

Backlog grows when the work outpaces the people, parts, or budget available to execute it. Confirm capacity before tuning anything else — schedule changes can't compensate for a missing technician.

What to look for

  • HIGHInsufficient maintenance technicians to handle current PM workload
  • MEDSkilled labor unavailable for specialized PM tasks (instrumentation, hydraulics, controls)
  • HIGHCompeting priorities between reactive repairs and preventive work
  • MEDVacation and sick leave reducing available workforce, especially in tight seasons

How to confirm in Maximo

  • Compare LABOR.AVAILHRS against forecasted PM hours per crew per week
  • Run the Labor utilization KPI and look for sustained >90% utilization across two pay periods

What to look for

  • HIGHRequired spare parts not in inventory at the time of WO release
  • MEDLong lead times for critical components against PM frequency
  • MEDBudget constraints limiting parts procurement mid-cycle
  • MEDSupply chain disruptions affecting OEM-only parts

Scheduling & planning issues

If the calendar isn't honest about capacity, the system will keep issuing work that can't be done. These are the configuration knobs that quietly produce a permanently growing backlog.

  • HIGHPM schedules too aggressive for available resources
  • HIGHMeter-based PMs not aligned with actual asset usage
  • MEDTime-based frequencies not optimized for asset criticality
  • MEDOverlapping PM schedules creating bottlenecks on shared crews
  • CRITCron task not running properly — check CRONTASKINSTANCE status
  • HIGHIncorrect cron task configuration (LeadTime, MaxWOs, GenForRoute parameters)
  • CRITPM work orders not generating on schedule
  • HIGHForecast dates not calculating correctly after meter resets
  • MEDActive days set incorrectly — e.g. Mon–Fri only when weekend coverage exists
  • MEDHoliday calendars not properly maintained for the current year
  • MEDSeasonal restrictions causing PM backlog to spike in shoulder months

Work order management issues

PMs that generate fine but never get assigned, approved, or completed on time will show up as overdue regardless of how clean the upstream schedule is.

  • HIGHWork orders stuck in approval workflows past 48h
  • HIGHSupervisors not reviewing or approving PMs on time
  • MEDMissing approval permissions in restricted SaaS configurations
  • HIGHWork orders generated but not assigned to technicians
  • MEDUnclear ownership of recurring PM tasks across crews
  • CRITAssignment application not available — a critical gap in restricted-tier SaaS
  • MEDPMs started but not completed on time
  • MEDJob plans too complex or time-consuming for the allotted window
  • HIGHTechnicians prioritizing emergency work over scheduled PMs

System configuration issues

Configuration that drifts away from the original PM design will quietly skip cycles or generate the wrong work at the wrong time.

  • HIGHExtended dates set too far out, skipping PM cycles
  • MEDNo proper tracking of deferred maintenance against asset criticality
  • MEDExtension dates causing sequence disruptions on multi-step routes
  • HIGHPM counters not incremented when skipping sequences
  • MEDMeter readings not updated regularly on automated routes
  • MEDDuplicate PM records generated for the same asset
  • MEDLead Time (Days) field on PM overriding organizational defaults
  • HIGHInsufficient lead time for planning and parts procurement
  • HIGHWork orders generated too late for timely execution

Data quality problems

PM compliance is a data problem before it is a process problem. Verify the foundation before tuning frequencies.

  • MEDAssets assigned to non-existent or retired locations
  • MEDAsset hierarchies not properly maintained after reorganizations
  • HIGHMeter readings not recorded consistently on metered PMs
  • HIGHPMs without detailed job plans attached
  • MEDUnclear or one-line task descriptions
  • MEDMissing material lists or tool requirements

Organizational & process issues

No tool fixes a culture that rewards firefighting. These factors are slower-moving but tend to be the durable causes when the others have been ruled out.

  • HIGHOrganization prioritizes "firefighting" over preventive work
  • MEDLack of management support for PM compliance targets
  • MEDNo consequences — formal or informal — for missing PM targets
  • HIGHPM schedules not based on reliability-centered maintenance (RCM)
  • MEDNo failure mode analysis (FMEA) to optimize PM tasks
  • MEDPM frequencies not validated against actual failure data
  • MEDTechnicians unaware of which PMs are critical this week
  • MEDNo shared visibility into PM backlog across crews
  • MEDLack of coordination between planners and executors at shift change

Technical & system issues

Constraints from the tier of Maximo you're on, or from integrations around it, can throttle PM execution in ways that don't show up in the PM module itself.

  • CRITSupervisors cannot create or directly manage work orders
  • CRITNo Assignment application for distributing work
  • HIGHNo Graphical Scheduling tools for visual planning
  • HIGHMissing Job Plans application for PM task definition
  • HIGHWork order data not syncing properly between Maximo and downstream systems
  • HIGHMobile app issues preventing field execution
  • MEDERP integration delays affecting parts availability

Measurement & reporting issues

Sometimes the backlog isn't really a backlog — the KPI itself is mis-defined or the people who need to see it can't act on it.

  • HIGHPM overdue threshold set too aggressively
  • MEDTarget metrics not aligned with organizational capacity
  • MEDCompliance measured incorrectly (e.g. counting closed-but-late as on-time)
  • HIGHSupervisors can only view but not act on overdue PMs
  • MEDNo real-time alerts for approaching due dates
  • MEDInadequate dashboard configuration

What to do, in order

Sequence matters. Stabilize before you optimize, then optimize before you transform.

IMMEDIATE — TODAY

Review PM backlog & triage

Sort overdue PMs by asset criticality, surface the top 5–10 risks, and confirm whether they're truly overdue or just past their threshold.

→ 01
SHORT-TERM — THIS WEEK

Verify PMWOGEN is running

Confirm the cron task instance is active, look at last run time, and check the row count of generated work orders against expectations.

→ 02
MEDIUM-TERM — THIS QUARTER

Optimize frequencies via RCM/FMEA

Use real failure data to confirm PM intervals are still appropriate; remove or reduce PMs that aren't reducing failure rates.

→ 03
LONG-TERM — NEXT YEAR

Move toward predictive maintenance

Reduce the PM burden by replacing fixed-interval inspections with sensor- or condition-based triggers where the economics support it.

→ 04
PROCESS

Establish escalation paths

Define who is notified, and when, as a PM approaches and passes its due date. Make sure each escalation step has a named owner.

→ 05
TRAINING

Equip supervisors

Confirm supervisors understand the full PM management workflow — generation, assignment, approval, completion, and counter behavior.

→ 06
TECHNOLOGY

Re-evaluate your tier

If your tier of Maximo lacks Assignment, Job Plans, or Graphical Scheduling, the backlog may be structural — consider upgrading.

→ 07
REMEMBER

It's almost never one thing

The root cause is usually a combination of these factors. Address the top 2–3 in parallel rather than waiting to "finish" one.

This guide is intended for planners, reliability engineers and supervisors operating a CMMS-based PM program. It does not replace vendor support documentation; for product-specific defects, follow your support contract.
